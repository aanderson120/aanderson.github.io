Bio

An English singer and songwritter who's debut album Lights debuted at No. 1 on the UK Albums Chart and sold over 850,000 copies in the UK. Her sound is a mixture of indie pop, sunthpop, folktronica, and indietronica. Her influences include Joni Mitchell, Kate Bush, Bjork, Amy Winehouse, Katy Perry, Lady Gaga, and Bon Iver.

Her phlianthropic works include working with the British Heart Foundation, Free the Children Charity, Children in Need, as well as joining the charity group Band Aid 30 in 2014. She is also an activist for homelessness. She has performed in the annual charity concerts for the "Streets of London" charity event at the Royal Albert Hall from years 2014 to 2018 consecutively. On 24 December 2015, Goulding volunteered in central London at the Marylebone Project to help end homelessness and to assist in the elimination of the stereotype associated with the homeless population.

On August 7th, 2018, Goulding and her boyfriend, Caspar Jopling, announced their engagement. They were married at York Minster on August 31st, 2019. Caspar Jopling is the grandson of former Conservative MP Michael Jopling, who was the Minister of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food from 1983 to 1987
